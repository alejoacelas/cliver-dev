# Measure 07 — Institution Affiliation (Low Scrutiny): Product Prioritization

## Selected stack

### 1. InCommon + eduGAIN federation IdP enumeration (`m07-incommon-edugain`)

Selected as the fast-pass baseline. This check builds a hashmap from publicly available SAML federation metadata (InCommon for US R&E, eduGAIN for ~80 international federations) and performs an O(1) lookup on the customer's email domain. Marginal cost is effectively $0; setup is $5–15k. The check's value is as a high-confidence positive signal: a match against a registered IdP scope means the domain is controlled by a genuine research-and-education institution, which catches all purpose-built-organization attacker stories (shell companies, CROs, dormant domains, community bio labs) at zero per-check cost. The audit trail is strong — XML-DSig-signed metadata is tamper-evident and re-verifiable years later. The check is structurally unable to distinguish individuals on federated domains (any email on `@stanford.edu` passes), but that limitation is appropriate for M07's low-scrutiny scope. The critical design insight is that this check works as a fast-pass, not a screen: federated matches skip further M07 review; non-federated domains proceed to the next check. Coverage is limited (~500 US InCommon members out of ~5,800 Title IV institutions, ~6,000 eduGAIN IdPs worldwide), but these are disproportionately the high-volume research institutions that generate most synthesis orders.

### 2. site:domain name search (`m07-google-site-search`)

Selected as the long-tail complement. For the majority of institutions not covered by federation metadata — including the ~91% of US higher-ed institutions outside InCommon, non-OECD universities, and the ~42–46% of the market that is commercial biotech/pharma — this check queries a web-search API for `site:<institution-domain> "<customer name>"` to determine whether the customer appears on their institution's website. It requires no per-institution adapter, works for any institution with a search-engine-indexed web presence, and costs $0.003–$0.045 per customer. The check's primary value is negative signal: zero results (`no_site_search_hits`) means the customer cannot be corroborated on their claimed institution's website, triggering manual review. This is the right fallback after federation lookup fails, because it covers the long tail without per-institution engineering investment. The vendor continuity risk (Google CSE sunsetting Jan 2027, Bing retired Aug 2025) is real but manageable — Brave Search API and SerpAPI are viable replacements. The check must be paired with institution-legitimacy verification (M09/M02) to avoid confirming attackers who control their own domains; the `site:` query searches the attacker's own content for purpose-built organizations, so the domain itself must be independently validated.

### 3. Visiting-scholar new-hire corroboration SOP (`m07-visiting-scholar-sop`)

Selected as the last-resort escalation for high-risk cases. When both the federation lookup and site-search return no signal and the customer claims a visiting/postdoc/new-hire role, this human-executed SOP provides the only remaining path to corroborate affiliation: web search for institutional mentions, sponsor-PI email confirmation, or research-visitors-office confirmation. At $10–$30 per case and 3–5+ business days latency, it is far too expensive for routine use, but it is the correct escalation for SOC-eligible orders where automated checks cannot produce a disposition. The SOP catches manufactured personas (IT admin alone without PI complicity), departed-member impersonation, and fabricated personas without directory entries. Its coverage gaps (50–70% PI non-response rate, 40–60% of non-US institutions lacking visitors offices) are real but acceptable because the SOP is designed to run on a small fraction of orders — only those that fail all automated checks and carry elevated risk signals.

## Dropped ideas

- **Institutional directory people-search scrape (`m07-directory-scrape`):** Per-institution HTML scrape adapters cost $200–$800 each to build, with $40K–$160K for the top 200 US R1+R2 institutions and ongoing maintenance for site redesigns. The signal it provides (directory presence) overlaps heavily with google-site-search at far higher engineering and maintenance cost. Its highest-value output — directory misses and role mismatches — is partially captured by the site-search check. The adapter model does not scale to the long tail, and GDPR constraints make EU coverage structurally unreliable. The marginal value over site-search does not justify the setup and maintenance burden.

- **Proxycurl LinkedIn person-lookup (`m07-proxycurl-linkedin`):** LinkedIn is self-asserted with no employment verification, making a matching profile a weak positive signal that provides no independent corroboration. Multiple attacker stories explicitly include LinkedIn profile creation as a free, minutes-long setup step. Coverage gaps are severe (~30–50% of OECD life-sciences academics lack LinkedIn, ~15–20% in LinkedIn-blocked countries). The legal risk from LinkedIn ToS and the unresolved *hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn* litigation is potentially blocking. The check's only value — catching naive attackers who neglect to create a profile — does not justify the legal exposure and the high false-negative review burden.

## Composition note

The three selected ideas compose into a tiered fast-pass/fallback/escalation pipeline:

- **Tier 1 — Federation lookup (automated, instant):** At order intake, extract the customer's email domain and look it up in the pre-built InCommon + eduGAIN scope index. If matched, fire `domain_in_incommon` or `domain_in_edugain` → M07 PASS. No further M07 checks needed. This resolves the highest-confidence population at zero marginal cost.
- **Tier 2 — Site search (automated, seconds):** For domains not in the federation index, query `site:<institution-domain> "<customer name>"` via Brave Search API or SerpAPI. Substantive hits (faculty page, lab page, publication, news article) → M07 PASS. Zero results → `no_site_search_hits` → manual review. Low-quality-only hits → `low_quality_hits_only` → spot-check. This tier requires that the institution domain has already been validated by M09/M02 to avoid confirming purpose-built organizations.
- **Tier 3 — Visiting-scholar SOP (manual, days):** For customers who fail both automated tiers and claim a visiting/postdoc/new-hire role on a SOC-eligible or first-ever order, trigger the 3-step human SOP (web corroboration → sponsor-PI email → visitors-office confirmation). At least one positive corroborator required; zero → deny or compliance escalation.
- **Shared dependency:** All three tiers depend on M09/M02 institution-legitimacy checks to establish that the institution domain belongs to a genuine third-party organization. Without this, tiers 1 and 2 confirm what an attacker plants on their own domain. The pipeline's output is a single measure-level M07 disposition per customer, with the tier that resolved it recorded in the audit trail.
