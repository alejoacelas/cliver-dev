# Measure 12 — Billing-Institution Association: Product Prioritization

## Selected stack

### 1. PSP AVS + Plaid Identity Match (`m12-psp-avs`)

Selected as the baseline. AVS is the cheapest possible M12 check: $0 incremental cost, since it is bundled into existing card-processing fees. The provider only needs to read response codes already present on the charge object. It fires on every card-paid order with zero setup beyond requiring billing address at checkout and reading the AVS fields. It catches the most common low-sophistication attack pattern — substituting a personal card for an institutional one (dormant-account-takeover Bypass D, inbox-compromise prepaid cards) — by detecting billing-address mismatches against issuer records. The structured AVS response codes (`pass`/`fail`/`unavailable`) slot cleanly into any rules engine. Plaid Identity Match extends coverage to ACH-paid orders at moderate per-call cost ($0.20-$1.00), though ACH volume at most synthesis providers is likely small enough that total spend is manageable. AVS has well-understood blind spots (inherited billing passes, invoice/PO orders invisible, international orders produce no signal), but these are addressed by the other selected checks rather than being reasons to skip the cheapest available signal.

### 2. Billing-shipping-institution consistency (`m12-billing-shipping-consistency`)

Selected for coverage depth on address-divergence attacks. This check fills a gap the PSP AVS synthesis explicitly flagged: AVS verifies billing-vs-issuer but never compares billing to shipping or to the institution's canonical address. The three-way address comparison catches account-hijack Method 2 (social-engineered shipping-address change to a drop location), which AVS misses entirely because the card itself is legitimate. It also catches dormant-account-takeover Bypass D from a different angle (residential billing address on an institutional order), providing redundant detection that strengthens confidence. The cost is near-zero with libpostal or $0.003-$0.009/order with Smarty. The main operational burden is false positives from multi-campus institutions and visiting researchers, but the exception-list mechanism (keyed on customer + address-triple) amortizes this over repeat orders. Critically, this check produces the explicit per-order audit artifact demonstrating billing-institution consistency was verified — the core M12 regulatory deliverable.

### 3. Fintech-neobank BIN denylist (`m12-fintech-denylist`)

Selected for LLC-branch coverage. The PSP AVS and billing-shipping checks are structurally blind to purpose-built organizations that align their addresses by construction. All seven LLC-based attacker branches (shell-company, shell-nonprofit, cro-framing, cro-identity-rotation, biotech-incubator-tenant, community-bio-lab-network, gradual-legitimacy-accumulation) use Mercury, Brex, Relay, or similar neobank rails — the exact BIN ranges this denylist targets. No other M12 idea addresses these branches. The check is cheap ($0-$0.07/check, $500-$2,000/year denylist maintenance) and integrates trivially via Stripe Radar inline rules or a BIN lookup at payment time. It cannot reliably block sophisticated shells (the reviewer faces a signal-to-noise problem distinguishing real small biotechs from shells), but it adds friction, creates an audit trail, and provides a composite signal that gains value when combined with m09/m18 institution-legitimacy checks. Without this check, the entire LLC-based attacker class passes M12 undetected.

## Dropped ideas

- **P-Card / commercial-card BIN positive signal (`m12-pcard-bin`):** Corroborative-only signal that actively produces false corroboration for the largest attacker class (all 7 LLC branches return `commercial` BINs). Third-party BIN data accuracy is only 32% vs Visa's own data (Pagos), and VBASS access is vendor-gated. The incremental value over AVS + fintech denylist is marginal: AVS already catches personal-card substitution, and the fintech denylist already flags neobank BINs. Adding a `pcard_positive` signal that validates shell companies is net-negative without access to VBASS-quality data.
- **Institutional supplier-onboarding verification (`m12-procurement-network`):** Strong signal when it fires (active supplier registration is hard to forge), but addressable scope is too narrow: US-only, invoice/PO-only, and only institutions on PaymentWorks/Jaggaer (~15-25% of customers by count). Setup cost is substantial (registering as supplier with hundreds of institutions, days-to-weeks approval per institution, annual renewal). SAM.gov's 10 req/day public-tier rate limit is not production-viable. PaymentWorks and Jaggaer have no public APIs — the operational pattern is manual CRM lookup by an AR analyst, making it a human process rather than a pluggable automated check. Better suited as an accounts-receivable business process than as a screening-pipeline component.

## Composition note

The three selected checks layer across payment types and attacker sophistication levels, sharing the PSP integration point:

- **Shared trigger:** All three fire at payment time on every card-paid order. AVS reads response codes from the charge object; the billing-shipping check normalizes the billing address from the same charge alongside the shipping and institution addresses; the fintech denylist checks the BIN from the same card. One PSP webhook or post-authorization hook feeds all three.
- **Execution model:** AVS runs inline during card authorization (no additional call). Billing-shipping consistency runs as a synchronous post-authorization step (address normalization + distance computation, sub-second). Fintech denylist runs as a BIN lookup against a local table (sub-millisecond). All three complete before the order enters the fulfillment queue.
- **Combined output:** Flags from all three checks roll up into a single M12 screening result per order. A `avs_zip_mismatch` + `billing_residential_on_institutional_order` combination is stronger than either alone. A `fintech_neobank_billing` flag on an order that passes AVS and billing-shipping consistency signals an LLC-based entity that warrants m09/m18 cross-referencing. The reviewer sees all M12 flags together and makes one disposition.
- **Coverage map:** AVS covers card-paid orders (US/CA/UK issuers). Billing-shipping consistency covers all orders with a billing address (card and some ACH). Fintech denylist covers card-paid orders from neobank issuers. Plaid Identity Match extends AVS-equivalent coverage to ACH. Invoice/PO orders remain the primary gap — these have no billing instrument to check and must rely on m09/m18 institution-legitimacy controls rather than M12 payment-method controls.
- **Audit trail:** Each check writes a structured record (AVS codes, triple-consistency distances, BIN lookup result) to a per-order screening log. Together they demonstrate the provider verified billing-institution association on every order, satisfying the M12 regulatory requirement.
