# Measure 04 — shipping-residential: Product prioritization

## Selected stack

### 1. m04-usps-rdi (USPS RDI residential indicator via Smarty)

USPS RDI is the foundation of the M04 stack. It delivers the authoritative US postal residential/commercial classification at negligible marginal cost ($0.001--$0.005 per lookup, or $0 incremental when the provider already calls Smarty for m03's CMRA check). The same API response serves both m03 and m04, which makes this the highest-pluggability idea in the measure: zero additional integration work for providers that already run m03. The interface is a single field (`metadata.rdi`) producing a single flag (`address_is_residential`), making it the clearest signal in the stack. It catches every residential-address bypass variant across all five mapped attacker stories for US addresses. Its structural limitation -- binary classification, US-only, no nuance beyond residential/commercial -- is precisely what makes it cheap and reliable. False positives (home-office founders, community-bio labs) are inherent to the measure's mandate and shared with every other M04 idea; RDI does not add unique FP burden. RDI should run on every US-addressed order as the first-pass residential gate.

### 2. m04-google-places-business (Google Places business presence)

Google Places adds two things RDI cannot provide: international coverage and semantic richness. At $0.032--$0.035 per check, it returns business types (`university`, `medical_lab`, `lodging`, `apartment_building`) that give reviewers context beyond a binary flag, reducing time-to-decision on flagged orders. For international addresses in OECD urban areas, it is the only M04 idea that produces any signal, partially closing the 40--55% international coverage gap that RDI and county assessor leave entirely open. It composes cleanly with RDI: for US addresses, a Places result corroborates or enriches the RDI flag; for international addresses, it stands alone. The dominant open issue is the Google Maps Platform ToS -- the restrictive clause appears to target the Places Aggregate API specifically (not the standard API), but legal review is required before deployment. If ToS review blocks this idea, the provider falls back to RDI-only and accepts the international gap. Recommended as a day-one integration alongside RDI, gated on legal clearance.

## Dropped ideas

- **m04-county-assessor (county assessor parcel use-code):** Identical hit/miss profile to RDI across all attacker stories; richer LBCS land-use codes rarely change reviewer decisions; cost is prohibitive for most providers ($80K/yr Regrid bulk or $0.05--$0.20/call ATTOM) relative to near-zero incremental detection value over RDI.

- **m04-str-coloc-sop (STR / Wayback co-location SOP):** Catches exactly one bypass variant (active Airbnb/VRBO STR as drop address) at $8--17/check in reviewer time; narrow hit surface does not justify the cost as a default check; providers facing a material foreign-institution STR-drop threat can add it as a conditional escalation without including it in the standard stack.

## Composition note

RDI and Google Places compose as a two-tier gate. RDI runs first on every US order at effectively zero marginal cost (piggybacking on the m03 Smarty call). Google Places runs on every order regardless of geography, providing the only M04 signal for international addresses and enriching the RDI binary for US addresses. When either check flags an address, the reviewer sees both results together in a single review step -- the RDI binary plus the Places business types, photos, and status -- avoiding the doubled-review-burden problem that would arise from running two binary checks independently. For orders where Google Places is blocked by ToS or returns no data (non-OECD rural, China), the provider accepts the gap at M04 and relies on downstream measures (m05 institutional address verification, m09 institution legitimacy) to catch threats that M04 cannot reach. The county assessor can be layered in later as an enrichment source for flagged US addresses if a provider acquires a Regrid license for other purposes, but it should not be purchased solely for M04. The STR SOP remains available as a reviewer playbook for the rare case where an upstream residential flag lands on an address in a tourist-market metro -- it requires no integration, only training.
