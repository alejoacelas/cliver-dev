## Section 2: Narrative

### What this check is and how it works

This check determines whether a US shipping address is on a residential parcel by querying the county assessor's land-use code through a national aggregator such as Regrid, ATTOM, or ReportAll. When a customer places an order, the system geocodes the shipping address and looks up the corresponding parcel. The aggregator returns a standardized use code (Regrid uses the LBCS classification system; ATTOM uses its own standardized property-use taxonomy). If the use code falls in the residential range, the system flags the order for human review. Mixed-use parcels receive a lower-priority flag. If the county is not covered or the use-code field is missing, the system degrades to the USPS RDI binary residential/commercial fallback. The check is US-only; international addresses produce no signal.

### What it catches

The check catches the cheapest residential-framing bypass variants across several attacker stories. In the community-bio-lab-network story, it fires on the garage-lab-at-home configuration (cost to attacker: ~$50–$100). In the dormant-domain story, it catches the $0-marginal residential colocation path. For foreign-institution Method 4, it catches US-based residential home offices. For cro-framing and gradual-legitimacy-accumulation, it catches the edge cases where attackers happen to ship to residential addresses. In each case, the check raises the attacker's minimum cost by forcing them off the residential path and onto a commercial address ($60–$500/month for a maker space, coworking desk, or virtual office).

### What it misses

Every commercial-address bypass method passes this check cleanly. Virtual offices, executive suites, coworking spaces, maker spaces, biotech incubator benches, and freight forwarders all sit on commercially-coded parcels and produce no signal. This is structural: the check distinguishes residential from non-residential, nothing more. The stage 5 hardening assessment classified this as Moderate rather than Critical because M04's mandate is residential detection, and commercial-address bypasses are addressed by other measures (M03 CMRA detection, M05 institution-association checks). The check also provides zero coverage for international addresses, which represent an estimated 40–55% of synthesis orders by count. The community-bio carve-out policy creates an inherent pass-through for the community-bio-lab-network story: the attacker's framing is by construction a "credible community-bio framing," so whether the reviewer catches the attacker depends on signals outside this check.

### What it costs

Marginal cost per check depends heavily on vendor and volume. Regrid's bulk license starts at $80K/year, which amortizes to ~$0.0005/check at very high volume but ~$1.60/check for a small provider doing 50K orders/year. ATTOM's entry plan is ~$95/month with metered quotas; per-call cost is estimated at $0.05–$0.20. ReportAll pricing is vendor-gated. Setup cost is approximately one engineer-week per vendor integration. For small synthesis providers, the Regrid bulk license is likely cost-prohibitive; ATTOM's per-call model is more accessible but less feature-rich.

### Operational realism

When `parcel_use_residential` fires, a reviewer pulls the parcel record and cross-checks the owner name against the customer's claimed institution. If the owner is the customer or a named individual rather than an institution, that strongly confirms residential use. The playbook includes a community-bio carve-out: if the customer presents a credible community-bio framing (e.g., an LLC with a community-bio name), the case escalates to a senior reviewer rather than auto-denying. This carve-out is necessary to avoid rejecting legitimate DIY-bio customers but creates the structural ambiguity noted above. Every disposition is documented in the order's compliance file with the parcel ID, use code, and full JSON response, providing a clear audit trail. Estimated reviewer time per flagged order is $8–17 based on SOP estimates from related ideas.

### Open questions

The ATTOM pricing claim ($95/month) is sourced from a third-party listing (Datarade) and may be stale — ATTOM's own pricing page is gated. Regrid's per-call REST pricing and rate limits are vendor-gated and would require a sales conversation to confirm. The ToS question — whether Regrid permits KYC-style screening use under its standard license — was searched for but not resolved. The use-code attribute completeness rate across US parcels is estimated at 85–95% but lacks external citation; the actual fraction of parcels where this check degrades to RDI fallback is unknown.

## Section 3: Open issues for human review

- **[vendor-gated] Regrid per-call REST pricing:** Public pages quote only bulk ($80K/yr) and Typeahead ($0.001/req). Per-call pricing for the Parcel API requires a sales contact. This directly affects marginal-cost-per-check estimates for non-bulk buyers.
- **[vendor-gated] ATTOM rate limits and exact pricing:** Rate limits visible only in the account console. The $95/month figure is from a third-party listing (Datarade), not ATTOM's own documentation (04C flagged as OVERSTATED-MILD).
- **[vendor-gated] ReportAll pricing:** Listed on request only.
- **[unknown] Regrid ToS for KYC-style screening use:** Searched for but not found. A synthesis provider would need to confirm with Regrid that using parcel data for customer screening is within the acceptable-use policy.
- **[unknown] Regrid use-code attribute completeness rate:** Estimated 85–95% nationally (best guess); no external citation. The fraction of parcels where this check produces no signal and falls through to RDI is operationally important but unquantified.
- **Structural: community-bio carve-out ambiguity.** The manual review carve-out for community-bio framing is inherently exploitable by the community-bio-lab-network attacker story, since the attacker's framing is by construction "credible." Resolution depends on signals outside this check.
- **04F flag: thin-search on ToS.** The `[unknown]` for Regrid ToS had only two search queries. A v2 research pass could expand the search.
- **06F flag: thin proxy for DIY-bio population size.** The 500–3,000 individual practitioner estimate and the <1% order share figure lack strong external grounding.
- **06F flag: use-code completeness estimate (85–95%) lacks derivation inputs.** No Regrid-published aggregate figure was found.
