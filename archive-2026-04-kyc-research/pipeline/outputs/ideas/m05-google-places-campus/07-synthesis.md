## Section 2: Narrative

### What this check is and how it works

This check verifies that a customer's shipping address is geographically inside the campus polygon of their claimed institution. It works in three steps: (1) resolve the institution name to a Google Places place_id via Text Search, (2) query the OpenStreetMap Overpass API for the campus boundary relation tagged as a university, hospital, or educational land use, and (3) geocode the shipping address via Nominatim and run a point-in-polygon containment test. If the address falls inside the polygon, the check produces a strong positive signal and the order clears this dimension. If outside, the system flags for review with the distance to the polygon edge. If no polygon exists for the institution, the check degrades to a manual fallback or routes to ROR/GLEIF address comparison. The check requires self-hosting Nominatim and Overpass for production volumes, since the public instances have strict rate limits (Nominatim: 1 req/sec absolute max).

### What it catches

The check is effective against attacker stories where the adversary claims affiliation with a known institution but ships to an address geographically distant from that institution's campus. It catches virtual offices and CMRAs in an institution's metro that are outside the campus polygon (inbox-compromise, cro-framing with a claimed institution, visiting-researcher off-campus path). It catches social-engineered address changes to off-campus drops (account-hijack). For the dormant-domain story, it catches cases where the revived lab had a real campus location and the attacker's new address is elsewhere. For the foreign-institution story, it catches foreign freight forwarders, residential addresses, and virtual offices outside the claimed institution's polygon — provided the polygon exists in OSM.

### What it misses

The check has three structural blind spots. First, it cannot detect attackers operating from inside real institutions: the biotech-incubator-tenant passes because the incubator is inside a biotech-district polygon, the it-persona-manufacturing attacker ships to the host lab building on campus, and the visiting-researcher ships to the host lab address. Second, carrier-level redirects (UPS My Choice, FedEx Delivery Manager, USPS COA) happen after the check passes during transit and are invisible to any order-time address check. Third, when an attacker claims their own entity (an independent CRO LLC, a shell nonprofit, a community-bio LLC) rather than a known institution, there is no polygon to test against — the check fires `polygon_missing_for_institution` and falls through to weaker fallbacks. The stage 5 hardening assessment classified all of these as Moderate, noting they are structural rather than implementation failures.

### What it costs

The Google Places Text Search call costs ~$0.032 per institution lookup, and the result (place_id) is cacheable so repeat customers from the same institution cost $0. Overpass and Nominatim are free if self-hosted. The dominant cost is infrastructure: self-hosting Nominatim and Overpass requires ~1–2 engineer-weeks of initial setup and approximately $200/month in ongoing infrastructure. This is a moderate upfront investment but low marginal cost. The legal review of Google Maps Platform ToS (same risk as m04-google-places-business) is an additional gating cost.

### Operational realism

The manual review playbook is well-structured: when `address_outside_campus_polygon` fires, the reviewer checks the distance from the polygon edge and looks for known affiliate buildings within 500m. For institutions with satellite campuses, the reviewer consults the institution's official locations page and ROR records. This is pragmatic but adds reviewer time for the multi-campus false-positive population (estimated 10–20% of orders from large research universities). The `polygon_missing_for_institution` path is more costly: the reviewer must manually construct a fallback bounding box, which defeats the purpose of automated containment checking. For industry customers (~30–45% of orders), the check provides no signal at all — it was designed for academic/hospital institutions. The audit trail includes the matched place_id, OSM relation ID, geocoded coordinates, and the containment result.

### Open questions

The OSM polygon availability estimate (50–60% of synthesis-relevant institutions globally) is central to the idea's value proposition but lacks a direct citation. The Nature Communications study cited in stage 6 addresses OSM building footprint completeness broadly but not university boundary relations specifically. The ~80% figure for US R1 universities is a pure best guess from the implementation with no source. Polygon availability for the specific institution types targeted by the foreign-institution story (non-Anglophone mid-tier institutions) is likely much lower. The OBFS field station count (~200+ in the US) is cited as general knowledge without a URL. Whether the polygon check should be re-evaluated on every order vs. only at registration is unspecified — the hardening assessment flagged this as a Minor finding.

## Section 3: Open issues for human review

- **Structural: industry customers (~30–45% of orders) receive no signal.** The check is fundamentally academic/hospital-oriented. Industry customers have no campus polygons. This is by design but means the check's value is limited to roughly half the customer base.
- **Structural: carrier-level redirect is a cross-cutting gap.** No M05 address-association check can detect UPS My Choice / FedEx Delivery Manager / USPS COA diversions that occur after order approval during transit. This affects credential-compromise, it-persona-manufacturing, and account-hijack stories.
- **Structural: biotech-incubator-tenant passes positively.** The attacker IS at a real biotech address inside a polygon. The complementary check is m05-incubator-tenant.
- **06F key flag: OSM polygon availability estimate (50–60%) lacks direct citation.** This is the binding constraint on the check's value and the number is weakly grounded.
- **04C MISSING-CITATION: "~80% US R1 universities have boundary relations"** is a pure best guess with no source. Should be reframed.
- **04C UPGRADE-SUGGESTED: Overpass query limits (100MB / 180s)** — widely reported but not directly cited from the Overpass API wiki.
- **04C: Places ToS concern** — same OVERSTATED issue as m04-google-places-business (clause targets Places Aggregate API, not standard Places API). Needs legal opinion.
- **Stage 5 Finding 3: polygon check re-evaluation cadence unspecified.** The implementation does not state whether containment is re-tested on every order or only at registration. Should be every order to catch address changes.
- **06F flag: OBFS field station count (~200+)** stated as general knowledge without a URL.
- **[unknown] Nominatim self-hosted hardware requirements.** Searched for but not found for 2026. Estimated ~1TB disk + multi-day import.
- **Stage 5 Finding 2: non-OECD polygon coverage is the weakest link for the foreign-institution story.** The specific institutions most likely to be exploited have the poorest OSM coverage.
