## Section 2: Narrative

### What this check is and how it works

This check operates an internal rules engine that runs on every order, comparing three addresses: the billing address (from the PSP's card authorization response), the shipping address (from the order form), and the institution's canonical address (from the m05/m18 institution-resolution pipeline). All three addresses are normalized using either libpostal (open source, free, in-process) or Smarty (commercial, roughly $0.003-$0.009 per order for US addresses). The engine computes pairwise haversine distances between geocoded points and applies a tiered rule: same country and same metro area (within 50 km) passes; same country but distinct metro triggers a soft flag for review; different countries or separations exceeding 1,000 km trigger a hard flag. Additional subflags detect specific patterns, such as a residential billing address on an institutional order (suggesting a personal card) or a billing-institution postal mismatch (suggesting the dormant-account-takeover substituted-card pattern).

### What it catches

The check catches geographic divergence between billing, shipping, and institutional addresses. Among the attacker stories in the m12 mapping, it catches dormant-account-takeover Bypass D (where the attacker falls back to a personal card whose billing address diverges from the institution), account-hijack Method 2 (where the attacker social-engineers a shipping address change to a drop location far from the institution), and partially catches dormant-domain personal-card paths and credential-compromise cloned-card paths where the billing address does not align with the institution. The check's structural contribution is that it forces attackers to either use inherited institutional billing (time-limited, traceable) or engineer geographic proximity — both of which constrain the attacker's options.

### What it misses

The check has three classes of structural bypass. First, inherited institutional billing: when an attacker uses a legitimate institutional payment method (PO, P-card, invoice), all three addresses are genuinely institutional and the triple passes by construction. This covers dormant-account-takeover Bypasses A-C, account-hijack inherited payment, and credential-compromise invoiced orders. Second, geographic-proximity target selection: the inbox-compromise branch's primary method selects target institutions within driving distance of the attacker's residence, ensuring the address triple is geographically consistent. The check passes because it detects geographic divergence, not identity divergence. Third, purpose-built organizations: all seven LLC-based branches (shell-company, shell-nonprofit, and five others) open real bank accounts whose billing addresses match their registered institutional addresses, satisfying the check by construction. Stage 5 classified these as Moderate findings because they are structural limitations of address-consistency checks rather than fixable implementation gaps, and they are addressed by other measures (m09 institution-legitimacy, m18 accreditation, M14/M20 identity verification).

### What it costs

Per-check costs are minimal: $0 with libpostal or $0.003-$0.009 per order with Smarty US, plus $0.0045-$0.009 for international addresses via Smarty International. Setup requires approximately 1-2 weeks of engineering for the rules engine, order-pipeline integration, and threshold tuning against historical legitimate-order data. The dominant ongoing cost is reviewer labor on false positives. The false-positive surface is wide and structurally unavoidable: multi-campus universities and national labs (plausibly up to 20% of orders at risk of address divergence), visiting researchers and sabbatical scholars (2-5% of the academic customer base), and distributed pharma/biotech companies (a meaningful share of commercial orders). At 15 minutes per reviewed case, reviewer labor could be substantial if distance thresholds are not well-tuned and exception lists not actively maintained.

### Operational realism

When a flag fires, the reviewer pulls the triple-consistency record showing normalized addresses, pairwise distances, and which subrule triggered. The reviewer checks whether the institution is multi-campus, whether the shipping address is an authorized off-site location, and whether the customer has ordered with the same address triple before. If needed, the reviewer contacts the customer to explain the divergence. Approved overrides feed an exception list keyed on (customer, address-triple) so subsequent orders skip the flag. The audit trail includes the triple-consistency record per order, reviewer dispositions, and an aggregate dashboard tracking flag rates and false-positive rates — the calibration signal for threshold tuning. This check produces the explicit artifact demonstrating the provider verified billing-institution consistency on every order, which is the core M12 regulatory requirement.

### Open questions

The claim check flagged a mild overstatement about the USPS Web Tools API: while the legacy API was retired in early 2026, a successor USPS APIs platform exists. The practical implication is minor since Smarty and libpostal are the recommended paths. Stage 5's Finding 3 (Minor) noted that the check's effectiveness against dormant-domain Bypass B depends on how m05/m18 resolves the institution canonical address for defunct/reanimated entities — this dependency is stated but not pinned down. Finding 4 (Minor) observed that adding cardholder-name-vs-institution-name matching to the rules engine would catch additional bypass paths (dormant-domain Bypass A, credential-compromise cloned card) but this is a scope expansion beyond address consistency. The 40% multi-campus-system estimate in the coverage analysis is a [best guess] without direct citation.
