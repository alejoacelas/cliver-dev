## Section 2: Narrative

### What this check is and how it works

This check searches PubMed (via NCBI E-utilities) and bioRxiv for publications affiliated with the customer's claimed institution in the last 5 years. The system uses the PubMed `[ad]` affiliation field tag to search the unstructured author-supplied affiliation strings in the PubMed database, trying the institution name and common variants ("University of X", "X Institute", etc.). It also queries the bioRxiv API across the same date range. The output is a count of publications per year and a sample of recent papers with DOIs and author lists. The system evaluates three flags: `no_pubmed_affiliation_5yr` (fewer than 5 PubMed papers in 5 years), `affiliation_collision_risk` (multiple distinct addresses appearing for the same institution name, suggesting name-collision noise), and `pubmed_thin_biorxiv_present` (bioRxiv-only hits with no PubMed-indexed publications). Both APIs are free; the total cost is $0 marginal with ~3–5 engineering days of setup.

### What it catches

For established, Anglophone, academically active institutions — major universities, large research nonprofits, established biotech companies with publication records — this check provides one of the strongest positive signals in the M09 suite. A robust multi-year publication record is expensive and time-consuming to fabricate. The check correctly identifies the bioRxiv-only pattern (used by shell-company and shell-nonprofit attackers who post 1–2 cheap preprints) as a weak signal. For the **cro-framing** and **biotech-incubator-tenant** stories, `no_pubmed_affiliation_5yr` fires — though these are also the customer classes where absence of publications is most common among legitimate entities.

### What it misses

The check has three significant bypass paths. First, the **gradual-legitimacy-accumulation** branch can create genuine PubMed-indexed publications by publishing in legitimate open-access journals ($1,500–$3,000 APC at PLOS ONE or Frontiers) or purchasing paper-mill authorship slots ($200–$5,000). These create real PubMed records indistinguishable from earned publications — the check has no mechanism to detect bought versus earned authorship. Second, the **dormant-domain** attacker benefits from the defunct entity's historical publication trail: real papers affiliated with the entity remain in PubMed indefinitely, and the check does not analyze recency trends to detect a step-function decline in output. Third, the **foreign-institution** story is entirely outside scope — the check validates the real institution's publication record, but the attacker's exploit is at the individual-affiliation level. The `no_pubmed_affiliation_5yr` flag is also structurally noisy for CROs (30–50% of commercial customers never publish), new institutions, non-Anglophone institutions, and community bio labs.

### What it costs

The check is free: both NCBI E-utilities and the bioRxiv API charge nothing. Setup cost is approximately 3–5 engineering days for the e-utility wrapper, name normalization logic, and optionally a bioRxiv mirror (a few GB, recommended for high-volume screening). The rate limit with a free NCBI API key is 10 requests/second, more than sufficient for screening operations. The bioRxiv API has no documented rate limit.

### Operational realism

The reviewer receives publication counts per year and a sample of papers. The strongest positive disposition is straightforward: >10 PubMed papers in 5 years with consistent affiliation means the institution has a real, sustained research presence. The `affiliation_collision_risk` flag requires the most reviewer effort: the reviewer must read 2–3 papers' full affiliation strings to confirm the address matches the customer's claimed address, guarding against the shell-nonprofit name-collision pattern. The `no_pubmed_affiliation_5yr` flag is high-volume and low-specificity — it fires on legitimate CROs, new startups, community labs, and non-Anglophone institutions at the same rate as on shells. It must be treated as a soft signal requiring corroboration, not a standalone indicator.

### Open questions

The hardening report identified two Moderate findings. First, the pay-to-publish/paper-mill bypass creates genuine PubMed records for $1,500–$5,000; a partial mitigation would be a `pubmed_single_paper_only` flag for institutions with exactly 1 PubMed hit, but an attacker willing to spend $3,000–$15,000 on 3–5 publications would defeat this. Second, the dormant-domain historical-publication bypass could be partially addressed by a recency-trend analysis (flag `pubmed_publication_trend_declining` if publications clustered 3–5 years ago with nothing in last 2 years). Neither suggestion was implemented. The 04C claim check noted an UPGRADE-SUGGESTED flag for verifying the `[ad]` tag against the PubMed help page, and that the author-homonym statistic ("two-thirds of PubMed author names ambiguous") should be cited to its original source.

## Section 3: Open issues for human review

- **No surviving Critical hardening findings.** All four findings were Moderate or Minor.
- **`[unknown]` fields:**
  - bioRxiv API rate limit — searched for 2 queries, no documented limit found.
  - Bioinformatics/computational biology PubMed coverage overlap — searched for 2 queries, no definitive statistics. Est. 10–20% of institutions with significant output outside PubMed (weakly grounded).
- **`[best guess]` fields with weak derivation:**
  - CRO/industry non-publication rate (30–50% of commercial customers) — reasonable estimate but no direct survey data.
  - Non-Anglophone institution PubMed invisibility (50–80% of output) — derived from PubMed English-language dominance statistics (86.5%) and Chinese journal English-publication rate (33%), but individual-institution impact varies.
  - Government/defense lab count (~40 US national labs/FFRDCs) — CITATION-MISSING, approximately correct but unsourced.
- **04C claim check unresolved flags:**
  - UPGRADE-SUGGESTED: Verify `[ad]` tag against PubMed help page directly.
  - Author-homonym statistic sourced from attacker mapping context, not primary literature — should be cited to original source.
- **Hardening suggestions not implemented:**
  - Finding 1 (Moderate): Add `pubmed_single_paper_only` flag for institutions with exactly 1 PubMed hit. Partial mitigation for pay-to-publish bypass; defeated by attacker spending $3,000–$15,000 on multiple papers.
  - Finding 2 (Moderate): Add `pubmed_publication_trend_declining` flag to detect step-function decline in publication output (dormant-domain historical-trail bypass).
- **Structural limitation acknowledged:** This check provides strong positive signal for established academic institutions but weak/no signal for the majority of non-academic commercial customers (~47% of market), new institutions, and non-Anglophone institutions. It must be treated as a **positive-evidence signal** (publications present is reassuring) not a **negative-evidence signal** (publications absent is not diagnostic). The `no_pubmed_affiliation_5yr` flag should carry very low weight unless corroborated by other M09 shell-pattern signals.
