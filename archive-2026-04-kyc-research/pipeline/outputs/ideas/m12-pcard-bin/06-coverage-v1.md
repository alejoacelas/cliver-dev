# Coverage research: P-Card / commercial-card BIN positive signal

## Coverage gaps

### Gap 1: Institutional researchers paying with personal credit cards (expense reimbursement)
- **Category:** Researchers at universities, government labs, or companies who use their personal consumer credit or debit card to pay for synthesis orders out of pocket and then seek reimbursement from their institution. These legitimate institutional customers have a consumer-category card, not a commercial/purchasing card.
- **Estimated size:** University procurement policies generally prefer or require P-cards or purchase orders for lab supplies, but many institutions permit personal-card purchases with reimbursement for small orders. [best guess: 15–30% of academic synthesis orders are paid by personal card with reimbursement, based on the prevalence of this practice for low-value lab consumables and the fact that not all labs have P-card access. The fraction is higher for small or less-administered labs.] [unknown — searched for: "researchers personal credit card lab supplies expense reimbursement percentage academic purchasing" — no aggregate statistic found].
- **Behavior of the check on this category:** false-positive (`pcard_consumer_on_institutional_claim` flag fires; reviewer asks for PO or institutional invoice)
- **Reasoning:** This is the primary false-positive class and the reason the implementation doc marks this idea as "corroborative only, never blocking." The reviewer friction is justified only if the signal is combined with other M12 checks.

### Gap 2: BIN-attribute data does not reliably distinguish consumer from commercial for all issuers
- **Category:** Cards where the BIN-attribute lookup returns incomplete or inaccurate product-type information — either `unknown` category or a misclassified consumer/commercial flag. Third-party BIN databases have limited accuracy: VBASS documentation notes that only 32% of third-party BIN data was accurate compared to Visa's own data [source](https://pagos.ai/learn/bin-data).
- **Estimated size:** If using a third-party aggregator (BinDB, binlist.net), the category field may be missing or wrong for a substantial fraction of BINs. [best guess: 10–20% of BINs in a third-party database may lack a reliable consumer/commercial classification. VBASS and Mastercard's own APIs are more accurate but are vendor-gated and require contractual access.] The implementation doc's `pcard_unknown_bin` flag handles this by falling back to other M12 signals.
- **Behavior of the check on this category:** no-signal (`pcard_unknown_bin` — the check provides no information and falls through)
- **Reasoning:** The accuracy of the check is directly gated by the BIN data source. If the provider cannot access VBASS or Mastercard's API (both require enterprise relationships), they must use a third-party database with lower accuracy. This is a structural limitation.

### Gap 3: Virtual card numbers (VCNs) from institutional P-card platforms
- **Category:** Institutional customers using single-use virtual card numbers generated by P-card platforms (JPM Single-Use Account, US Bank PaymentNet ePayables, Citi Virtual Card Accounts). The VCN BIN may not match the parent P-card BIN and may not appear as "commercial/purchasing" in BIN databases.
- **Estimated size:** VCN usage in institutional procurement is growing. [best guess: 5–10% of institutional P-card transactions now use VCNs, based on the push toward ePayables in university and government procurement.] [unknown — searched for: "virtual card number institutional procurement percentage" — not directly searched]. The implementation doc flags this as a known issue with the note that VCN BINs are "documented internally to issuer, not in public databases."
- **Behavior of the check on this category:** no-signal or false-negative (VCN may resolve as `unknown` or `consumer` despite being an institutional purchasing instrument)
- **Reasoning:** This is an increasingly important gap as VCN adoption grows. The mitigation is access to issuer-level VCN BIN documentation, which is vendor-gated.

### Gap 4: Non-US institutional cards with unfamiliar issuer names
- **Category:** International institutional customers whose P-card or corporate card is issued by a non-US bank unfamiliar to the BIN lookup service. The card may correctly resolve as "commercial" but the issuer name is unrecognized, so the reviewer cannot confirm whether it is a legitimate institutional P-card program.
- **Estimated size:** ~45% of DNA synthesis revenue is non-US [source](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/dna-synthesis-market-109799). International institutional procurement often uses different card products (corporate T&E cards, institutional credit lines) that may not follow the US P-card model. [best guess: the majority of non-US institutional orders will not have a recognizable P-card BIN — the P-card model is primarily a US/Canada/UK phenomenon. So ~30–40% of all synthesis orders (non-US institutional) get no positive corroboration from this check.]
- **Behavior of the check on this category:** no-signal (the check provides no positive or negative signal for unfamiliar international issuers)
- **Reasoning:** This check's value is concentrated on US institutional customers. For international customers, the P-card positive signal is largely absent, and the check falls through to other M12 controls.

### Gap 5: Small businesses with commercial cards that are not institutional researchers
- **Category:** Non-research small businesses that have a commercial credit card (e.g., a Visa Business card from their local bank) and use it to make curiosity-driven or non-institutional DNA synthesis orders. The card resolves as "commercial" and generates a `pcard_positive` signal, but the buyer is not at the claimed institution — or is not an institution at all.
- **Estimated size:** [best guess: a small fraction of synthesis orders — perhaps 1–3% — come from non-institutional commercial cardholders. The synthesis customer base is heavily skewed toward academic and pharma, so the population is small. But the false-positive (in this case, a false-corroboration) creates a misleading positive signal.]
- **Behavior of the check on this category:** false-corroboration (the check reports `pcard_positive` for a non-institutional buyer)
- **Reasoning:** The BIN check alone establishes that the card is a commercial product, not that the cardholder is at the claimed institution. This is why the implementation doc marks the check as corroborative — it increases confidence in an independently-verified institutional claim, but should not be the sole basis for trust.

## Refined false-positive qualitative

1. **Personal-card institutional researchers** (Gap 1) — the dominant false-positive class. [best guess: 15–30% of academic orders.] The `pcard_consumer_on_institutional_claim` flag fires on every such order. This is manageable because the flag is soft (corroborative negative, not blocking).
2. **Small businesses with commercial cards** (Gap 5) — false corroboration. Low volume but misleading.
3. **Academic visitors using home-institution commercial card at a host institution** — mentioned in the implementation doc. Generates a "commercial card from issuer A, claimed institution B" pattern.

## Notes for stage 7 synthesis

- This idea is **corroborative, not dispositive.** Its primary value is providing a positive signal (`pcard_positive`) when a claimed-institutional order is backed by a recognized P-card. The absence of a P-card is the *normal case* for many legitimate customers (Gap 1), so the negative signal is weak.
- The check's value is US-centric (Gap 4). International institutional customers get no meaningful signal from this check. For a synthesis provider with a global customer base, this idea provides corroboration for roughly half the institutional-customer base.
- Data-source quality is the load-bearing variable (Gap 2). VBASS/Mastercard APIs provide accurate commercial/consumer classification but require enterprise relationships. Third-party databases are less reliable. The cost-accuracy tradeoff determines whether this check adds real signal or just noise.
- 48% of institutions reported increased P-card transactions in recent surveys [source](https://www.emburse.com/blog/a-complete-guide-to-p-cards), suggesting P-card adoption is growing, which improves this check's coverage over time.
