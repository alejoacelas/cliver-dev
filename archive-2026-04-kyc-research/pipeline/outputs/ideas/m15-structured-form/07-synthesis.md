## Section 2: Narrative

### What this check is and how it works

This check replaces the current free-text "intended use" field on synthesis orders with a structured form that uses controlled vocabularies. At order time, the customer selects from enumerated intended-use categories (vaccine development, diagnostic assay, protein expression, etc.), enters a host organism validated against the NCBI Taxonomy database (which auto-fills the canonical taxon name), declares the BSL containment level, and reports their IBC approval status. A validator runs consistency checks: the declared BSL must match the source organism's CDC BMBL risk group, select-agent orders must have IBC approval, and NCBI Taxonomy IDs must resolve. The form is not a verification layer — it is a data-capture layer designed to produce machine-checkable structured records that feed the higher-leverage M15 checks: m15-llm-extraction cross-references the structured fields against free text, m15-screening-reconciliation compares them against vendor screening outputs, and drift detectors track per-customer trajectories over the structured features.

### What it catches

The form forces a minimum level of domain-specific knowledge that casual or uninformed attackers are unlikely to possess. A student from an unrelated department cannot simply write "research purposes" — they must select a specific use category, enter a valid organism taxon ID, and declare a BSL level that is consistent with the organism's risk group. Getting these wrong triggers `bsl_inconsistent` or `taxid_invalid`; leaving them blank triggers `field_missing`. The `select_agent_no_ibc` flag catches select-agent orders without IBC approval. The `excessive_other` heuristic catches customers who try to fill the form with escape-hatch values. For bulk-order concealment, the per-order structured data makes SOC orders machine-distinguishable from non-SOC orders, enabling downstream reconciliation to flag specific order lines.

### What it misses

The form cannot detect malicious intent behind technically accurate data. A purpose-built CRO, a patient attacker accumulating legitimacy, or an insider with real protocol access fills the form correctly — their `intended_use_category`, `host_organism_taxid`, and `ibc_approval_status` are all valid. This affects eight attacker stories. The `other/unknown` escape hatch is the most operationally important gap: if customers routinely use it, the form collapses to a free-text equivalent. The `excessive_other` flag fires at 3+ fields, but an attacker using `other` on only the intended-use field (the single most important field) avoids the flag. The form is also NIH-centric: `ibc_approval_status` uses Section III-F vocabulary that doesn't map to UK, EU, or Australian biosafety frameworks, affecting ~20–30% of a global provider's customers. Multi-channel ordering (email, phone) may bypass the form entirely if it is not mandated for all channels.

### What it costs

Marginal cost per order is zero — the form is HTML rendering plus a database write. Setup cost is 3–6 engineer-weeks for form development, controlled-vocabulary curation (seeded from CDC BMBL and NCBI Taxonomy), NCBI lookup integration, validation logic, and reviewer-queue UI. Ongoing cost is curator effort to keep vocabularies current as new use categories and organisms emerge. The NCBI Taxonomy API is free and public with standard rate limits.

### Operational realism

The reviewer's task on flagged submissions is straightforward: for `field_missing` or `taxid_invalid`, request resubmission; for `bsl_inconsistent`, verify the source organism's risk group and determine whether the customer's declared BSL is defensible; for `excessive_other`, assess whether the escape-hatch usage reflects genuinely novel work or evasion. The form's primary value is not in its own flags but in the structured data it produces for downstream checks. Without the form, m15-llm-extraction cannot cross-reference structured fields against free text, m15-screening-reconciliation cannot compare vendor outputs against typed organism and use-category fields, and drift detection over customer order histories loses its features. The audit trail is the submitted form JSON, the NCBI lookup snapshot, the validator output, and the reviewer's disposition.

### Open questions

The escape-hatch usage rate is the single most important unknown. The best-guess range of 10–25% has no citation from synthesis-specific data. If the rate is at the high end, the form's value is substantially reduced. The `excessive_other` threshold of 3+ fields is arbitrary; the hardening review recommends per-field triggers for load-bearing fields (intended-use category and source organism). The multi-channel mandate is unspecified — if email or phone ordering bypasses the form, the structured data capture has gaps. The single-select constraint on `intended_use_category` forces lossy representations of multi-faceted projects; allowing multi-select would reduce false disagreements with downstream checks. The `ibc_approval_status` vocabulary needs extension to cover international biosafety frameworks.

## Section 3: Open issues for human review

- **No surviving Critical hardening findings.** All findings were Moderate or Minor.
- **Moderate finding M1 — The `other/unknown` escape hatch degrades the form.** The `excessive_other` threshold of 3+ fields is arbitrary; an attacker using `other` on only 1–2 fields (including `intended_use_category`) avoids the flag. Per-field triggers for load-bearing fields are recommended.
- **Moderate finding M2 — Form does not challenge exemption claims.** `ibc_approval_status = not-applicable` and `exempt-section-III-F` are valid selections that are captured but not verified. By design, verification depends on m15-ibc-attestation.
- **Moderate finding M3 — Multi-channel ordering may bypass the form.** The implementation does not specify whether the structured form is mandatory for all ordering channels (web portal, email, phone, EDI).
- **`[best guess]` fields requiring validation:** Escape-hatch usage rate (10–25% — no citation; load-bearing for the form's value proposition); codon-optimization prevalence (15–25% — no citation); multi-category project frequency (10–20% — no citation).
- **All 4C claims verified.** NCBI E-utilities, IGSC HSP v3.0, CDC BMBL citations all confirmed.
- **6F form check flags:** Gap 1 (escape-hatch usage) estimate is load-bearing and lacks citation; Gap 5 (codon optimization) prevalence could use a citation.
- **Minor finding N1 — Single-select intended-use for multi-purpose orders.** Multi-select or ranked primary/secondary categories recommended.
- **Minor finding N2 — Vocabulary drift.** No defined update cadence for controlled vocabularies.
- **Structural note:** The form is a precondition for higher-leverage M15 checks. Its coverage gaps primarily matter insofar as they degrade downstream checks (m15-llm-extraction, m15-screening-reconciliation).
