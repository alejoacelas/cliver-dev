# Measure 08 — institution-denied-parties

**No relevant stories found.**

Searched all 19 wg attacker branches under `attackers/source/` for any bypass method, choice of host institution, or scenario element that would have to defeat, evade, or pre-empt screening the customer's *institution* against a national or UN denied-parties list. None match. Across the branch set:

- Branches that operate at real existing institutions (`visiting-researcher`, `unrelated-dept-student`, `insider-recruitment`, `lab-manager-voucher`, `bulk-order-noise-cover`, `inbox-compromise`, `credential-compromise`, `account-hijack`, `dormant-account-takeover`, `it-persona-manufacturing`) all target US and European R1 universities, small US colleges, hospital systems, or core facilities — none of which are on denied-parties lists. The branches explicitly select institutions for their *clean* status (cleared address, established provider relationship, real publication trail).
- The `foreign-institution` branch is the only one operating at non-Anglophone universities, but its scenario explicitly excludes denied-parties targets: it operates at "Brazilian federal universities, Japanese prefectural labs, Indian state agricultural universities, Indonesian/Vietnamese national research institutes, Russian-language CIS institutions" — i.e., legitimate mid-tier R1 equivalents in countries with strong passports and clean export-control posture. The attacker's "differential advantage is operating where Western providers' verification stacks have limited reach," not where the institution itself is sanctioned.
- Purpose-built-organization branches (`shell-company`, `shell-nonprofit`, `cro-framing`, `cro-identity-rotation`, `biotech-incubator-tenant`, `gradual-legitimacy-accumulation`, `community-bio-lab-network`, `dormant-domain`) construct fresh, clean US LLCs / nonprofits with newly-chosen names. By construction these entities are not on any denied-parties list — and several branches explicitly note that their threat model is fooling *legitimacy* signals (research history, publications, IBC) rather than evading any list.
- The `dormant-domain` branch revives the canonical domain of a previously-defunct legitimate research entity ("a defunct research entity whose publication trail is still live in PubMed, Google Scholar, and NIH RePORTER"). Such entities were not on denied-parties lists when they existed and are not added after they go defunct.

A measure-08 bypass would require the attacker to operate from, or claim affiliation with, an institution that *is* on a national or UN denied-parties list and engineer past the screen. None of the wg branches model that adversary; they all model attackers whose host institutions trivially clear denied-parties screening by construction. If a future wg branch covers attackers at sanctioned institutes (e.g., Iranian or DPRK research institutes), this file should be revisited.
